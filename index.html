Tribute to Louis Pasteur

Here's a time line of Louis Pasteur:

  • 1822 - 27 December 1822 Born In Dole, France.
  • 1826 - The family moved to Marnoz.
  • 1827 - The family moved to Arbois.
  • 1831 - Pasteur entered primary school.
  • 1838 - He left for Paris to enroll in a boarding school, but became homesick and returned in November.
  • 1839 - He entered the Collège Royal at Besançon to study philosophy.
  • 1840 - Earned his Bachelor of Letters degree.
    - He was appointed a tutor at the Besançon college while continuing a degree science course with special mathematics.
  • 1841 - He failed his first examination.
  • 1842 - He managed to pass the baccalauréat scientifique (general science) degree from Dijon, where he earned his Bachelor of Science in Mathematics degree (Bachelier ès Sciences Mathématiques) in 1842, but with a mediocre grade in chemistry.
    - Later Pasteur took the entrance test for the École Normale Supérieure. During the test, he had to fight fatigue and only felt comfortable with physics and mathematics. He passed the first set of tests, but because his ranking was low, Pasteur decided not to continue and try again next year.
  • 1843 - He passed the test with a high ranking and entered the École Normale Supérieure.
  • 1845 - He received the licencié ès sciences degree.
  • 1846 - He was appointed professor of physics at the Collège de Tournon (now called Lycée Gabriel-Faure) in Ardèche. But the chemist Antoine Jérôme Balard wanted him back at the École Normale Supérieure as a graduate laboratory assistant (agrégé préparateur).
  • 1848 - He became professor of chemistry at the University of Strasbourg.
    - He resolved a problem concerning the nature of tartaric acid.
  • 1849 - He met and courted Marie Laurent, daughter of the university's rector.
    - He Married With Marie Laurent on 29 May 1849, and together had five children, only two of whom survived to adulthood; the other three died of typhoid.
  • 1850 - Pouchet stated that air everywhere could cause spontaneous generation of living organisms in liquids. In the late 1850s, he performed experiments and claimed that they were evidence of spontaneous generation.
  • 1865 - Pasteur patented the process, to fight the "diseases" of wine.
    - Pasteur went to Alès and worked for five years until 1870.
    - 2-year-old Camille died of a liver tumour.
  • 1866 to 1868 - Studies on Wine & Vinegar.
  • 1876 - Studies on Beer.
  • 1870 - He applied this immunization method to anthrax, which affected cattle, and aroused interest in combating other diseases.
    - Studies on Silk Worm Disease
  • 1881 - On 21 March, he announced successful vaccination of sheep.
  • 1885 - Pasteur produced the first vaccine for rabies by growing the virus in rabbits, and then weakening it by drying the affected nerve tissue.
    • 1886 - He treated 350 people, of which only one developed rabies. The treatment's success laid the foundations for the manufacture of many other vaccines. The first of the Pasteur Institutes was also built on the basis of this achievement.
  • 1891 - Pasteur Institute had been extended to different countries, and currently there are 32 institutes in 29 countries in various parts of the world.
  • 1894 - A stroke or uremia severely impaired his health.
  • 1895 - Failing to fully recover, he died on 28 September 1895, near Paris.
    - He was given a state funeral and was buried in the Cathedral of Notre Dame, but his remains were reinterred in the Pasteur Institute in Paris, in a vault covered in depictions of his accomplishments in Byzantine mosaics.

Awards and honours:

  • 1881 - Legion of Honor Grand Cross.
  • 1856 - Rumford Medal.
  • 1869 - Foreign Member of the Royal Society.
  • 1874 - Copley Medal.
  • 1882 - Albert Medal.
  • 1883 - Foreign Associate of the National Academy of Sciences.
  • 1889 - Cameron Prize for Therapeutics of the University of Edinburgh.
  • 1895 - Leeuwenhoek Medal .

"Take interest, I implore you, in those sacred dwellings which are designated by the expression term, laboratories. Demand that they be multiplied and advanced. These are the temples of the future, temples of well-being and happiness ... where humanity grows greater, stronger, better."

-- Louis Pasteur
